Heart Disease

Different things can cause serious heart conditions. But heart disease is the number one killer in the world. It is also a major cause of disability. There are many different forms of heart disease. The most common cause of heart disease is Narrowing or Blockage of the Coronary arteries, the blood vessels that supply blood to the heart itself. This is called coronary artery disease and happens slowly over time. It's the major reason people have heart attacks. Other heart conditions, such as those that affect your heart's muscle, valves or rhythm, also are considered forms of heart disease. Many forms of heart disease can be prevented or treated with healthy lifestyle choices.

Symptoms of Heart Disease

The symptoms of Heart disease include:

  • Chest pain
  • Weakness
  • Shortness of breath
  • Coldness in your legs or arms
  • Pain in the neck, jaw, throat
  • High blood pressure
  • Nausea and cold sweats
  • Stomach pain
  • Pain in upper abdomen or back
  • Irregular heartbeat
